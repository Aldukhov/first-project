Key Problems in Education

Neither in school nor in college are we taught how to properly study the material. We prepare for exams and memorize facts. We practice solving repetitive tasks to perform better on tests, but often it doesn't result in real knowledge. Rote memorization quickly fades and is not beneficial.

Conclusion: Learning is a skill that needs to be acquired, but for some reason, it's rarely taught. What can be done about it?

Specific techniques and exercises can change your approach to learning, making it effective and engaging. These techniques are applied in practice at Practicum.

Five Practices from Barbara Oakley

Two Types of Attention

Deep knowledge emerges when you alternate between focused and diffused thinking.

Recall

Recalling what you've learned helps connect scattered pieces of memory.

Interleaving

Studying multiple skills simultaneously enriches each other.

Questions

While listening to a teacher, create a good question that genuinely interests you.

Illusion of Competence

Repeat it to yourself, write it down, explain it to a friend: you only think you understand the new topic.

For Those Who Love to Procrastinate

Barbara Oakley's Story

From childhood, Barbara struggled with mathematics. She considered herself a complete humanitarian, and not the smartest one at that. In the army, she studied the Russian language to get a bonus, and she did it so successfully that she was promoted to a commander. But for career advancement, she needed to pass a math exam. That's when Barbara came up with her own approach to exact sciences. It turns out that if something is "difficult" for you, the knowledge you gain through hard work is much deeper than that of those who understand it immediately.
